Prabin Kumar Rath

I graduated with a Masters in Robotics and AI from Arizona State University in Spring 2024. I am currently working as a MLOps Engineer Intern at Experian. My interest lies in 3D Perception, Robot Learning, and Software Engineering for automation applications.

For my Master's thesis I worked on motion planning for robotic manipulators at Logos Lab with Dr. Nakul Gopalan. I developed deep-learning-based imitation learning algorithms that can zero-shot generalize across unseen commercial robotic arms. My work involved large-scale synthetic data generation on the cloud using simulators like PyBullet and NVIDIA IsaacSim. This data was used to train behavior cloning and 3D collision detection algorithms on GPU clusters. I am passionate about robot learning policies for manipulation, planning, and grasping. My long-term goal is to develop foundation models for robot learning that generalize to unseen environments and embodiments, enabling one-shot or few-shot learning from human demonstrations.

As a Research Assistant at ASU, I have also worked with Dr. Hongbin Yu at ASU-EVSTS Lab to develop robust and efficient detection and tracking algorithms with 128 channel LiDARs. My work focused on improving pedestrian safety at traffic intersections using infrastructure sensor setups.

Earlier I was a software developer and data analyst at Wells Fargo - EGS, where I worked on document layout analysis for numerical data extraction from handwritten forms. I received my B.Tech degree in Computer Science and Engineering from the National Institute of Technology, Rourkela in Spring 2020.

I am looking for a PhD position in robot learning starting in Fall 2025.
